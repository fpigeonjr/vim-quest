The Bug

The Wave 1 Gate shrine at tile (40, 22) was designed as the climax of Level 1 — the player must first destroy all 3 crates using x to prove mastery of the operator command before being allowed to pass.

However, the gate was implemented purely as a hint message. When the player stepped on the shrine tile before breaking the crates, the hint bar updated to say "The gate is blocked!" — but there were no actual wall tiles in the path. The player could simply walk past the shrine tile and continue east without ever touching a crate.

This turned the crate puzzle from a mandatory skill gate into an optional annoyance, completely undermining the progression design of Level 1.

A full vertical wall runs at col 41 from row 13 to row 31 — spanning the entire playable height between the river and the outer boundary wall. This prevents the player from walking around the barrier through open grass above or below the corridor. When gateUnlocked becomes true, openGate() iterates GATE_WALL_TILES (19 tiles) and calls setTileAt() on each, converting them to path and destroying their Arcade Physics colliders.

New Feature — Level 1 Completion Flag

A gold flag tile at (29, 6) — north of the river, directly above the bridge — marks the Level 1 win condition. It is accessible only after the bridge (cols 28–30) has been built with i. A wall enclosure (cols 27–31, rows 4–8) forces the player to approach from the south via the bridge path. Stepping on the flag triggers a confetti burst and a win overlay.

Design Notes

The initial fix placed walls only at (41,22) and (41,23) — spanning the 2-tile corridor. During manual testing it became clear the player could still walk off the path into the open grass and circle around the barrier. The fix was revised to a full vertical wall at col 41, rows 13–31 — from just below the river to just above the outer boundary wall. This covers every tile the player can physically reach east of the shrine.

The wall is placed one column east of the shrine (col 41, not 40). This keeps the shrine itself fully reachable so the hint fires and the player reads the objective before hitting the wall.

Remaining recommendation: A subtle visual effect (e.g. a brief camera shake or tile flash) when the gate opens would make this moment feel more satisfying. The mechanic is now correct; the feel could still be elevated.
